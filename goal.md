The primary goal of **Sentinel Grid** is to engineer an autonomous, decentralized tactical infrastructure that eliminates the "Death Gap"—that critical 10-to-15-minute window between an attack and the arrival of traditional police services. By leveraging **Kestra** as a high-speed orchestration engine, the project aims to reduce response times to under 60 seconds by mobilizing a "Swarm" of nearby, background-checked gig workers and night-shift responders who are already on the road. Unlike passive tracking apps that merely show a dot on a map, this system is designed to be offensive; it uses on-device **AI via Google Coral Edge TPUs** to verify distress in real-time, instantly triggering a localized "War Mode" that weaponizes the environment through sirens, strobe lights, and a coordinated human response.

Furthermore, Sentinel Grid seeks to solve the "Bystander Effect" by transforming public safety into a high-trust  **Emergency Economy** , where verified responders are incentivized with instant financial bounties delivered through automated smart-escrows. Trust is non-negotiable in this mesh, which is why the project automates "Zero-Crime" verification by cross-referencing every responder against the **Cybercrime Suspect Repository** to ensure the "Guardians" aren't predators themselves. Ultimately, the win condition is a complete psychological shift of the street: creating a reality where any act of violence is met with such immediate, AI-verified, and human-dense intervention that the "cost" of committing a crime becomes impossible for any gang to pay.
